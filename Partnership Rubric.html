How it works

Eight criteria, each scored 1–4 and weighted. Scores are summed and normalized to 100. Three criteria act as gates — a score of 1 on any gate flags the partnership for explicit discussion, regardless of total score.

What the total score means

The lowest possible score is 25 (all 1s). These bands interpret the weighted total.

25–50
Do not move forward Partnership is missing fundamentals. Pass, or kick back with specific feedback.
51–62
Probably not a great fit Meaningful gaps. Brief discussion to see if there's a stronger version, but default is a pass.
63–75
Explore further & push Real potential, not quite there. Dig into the weakest criteria and see if the partner can meet us higher.
76–100
Definitely worth exploring Strong fit across the board. Move it forward and start shaping the opportunity.

Criteria & weights

All eight, in priority order.

A note on using this

This rubric exists to structure discussion, not replace it. A high score doesn't auto-greenlight a partnership and a low score doesn't auto-kill one. The gates are here to make sure we explicitly debate the partnerships that miss something fundamental — not to reject them automatically.
